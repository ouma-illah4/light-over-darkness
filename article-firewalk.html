Walking Through the Fire

The Day of the Lord has come — not to destroy you,
but to refine you and bring you into resurrection life.

“I have refined you… in the furnace of affliction” — Isaiah 48:10

In the fierce wilderness of this world — where depression suffocates, addiction enslaves, anxiety storms, and emptiness screams — the fire feels unbearable. Yet this is the very place where God meets us. Through the Sacrificial Pattern, we see that our affliction is not the end. It is the path where Jesus walked first, and where we now look to His crucifixion and find breakthrough — just as the Israelites, dying from snakebites in the desert, were told to look at the bronze serpent and live.

1. Initiation – God Leads Us Into the Day of the Lord

The Day of the Lord is not only future glory; it is present refining. God Himself initiates the fire because He refuses to leave us in our sin and brokenness. Just as He led Israel into the wilderness after the Red Sea, He leads us through seasons of suffering so that we might be purified and prepared for resurrection life.

Scripture: “I have refined you, though not as silver; I have tested you in the furnace of affliction” (Isaiah 48:10). The fire is God’s loving initiation.

2. Representation – Jesus Enters the Fire First

Jesus is our perfect Representative. Before we ever felt the heat of our personal wilderness, He walked the ultimate Day of the Lord — the darkness of Gethsemane, the betrayal, the scourging, and the agony of the cross. On Calvary, the full wrath of the Day of the Lord fell upon the sinless Son so that it would not consume us.

Scripture: “He was pierced for our transgressions, he was crushed for our iniquities” (Isaiah 53:5). Jesus walked through the fire ahead of us.

3. Consecration & Substitution – Jesus Becomes the Bronze Serpent for Us

In the desert, when fiery serpents bit the people because of their unbelief, God told Moses to lift up a bronze serpent on a pole. Anyone who looked at it in faith was healed. Jesus Himself said, “Just as Moses lifted up the snake in the wilderness, so the Son of Man must be lifted up” (John 3:14). On the cross, Jesus became the substitutionary offering — the One who took the bite of sin and death so that we might look to Him and live.

Scripture: “God made him who had no sin to be sin for us, so that in him we might become the righteousness of God” (2 Corinthians 5:21).

4. Obedience & Self-Offering – Our Response in the Fire

The bronze serpent did not heal those who turned away; it healed only those who looked. In our affliction, God calls us to the same obedience: stop striving, stop hiding, and look to the crucified Christ. The greatest sacrifice we can offer is not our own strength, but our surrendered self — our pain, our failures, our addictions, our depression — laid at the foot of the cross.

Scripture: “Offer your bodies as a living sacrifice” (Romans 12:1). “To obey is better than sacrifice” (1 Samuel 15:22).

5. Transformation – Breakthrough and Resurrection Life

The Day of the Lord always ends in resurrection. The same fire that tested Israel and refined the remnant brought them into the Promised Land. The same cross that held Jesus in death became the door to life. When we look to Him in our affliction, the Holy Spirit brings breakthrough. What felt like the end becomes the beginning of new life — more than conquerors, filled with hope, and sent out as witnesses.

Scripture: “In this world you will have trouble. But take heart! I have overcome the world” (John 16:33). “We are more than conquerors through him who loved us” (Romans 8:37). “The Day of the Lord will come… but for you who revere my name, the sun of righteousness will rise with healing in its rays” (Malachi 4:1-2, echoing Micah’s promise of restoration).

Look to the One lifted up on the cross.
In the fire of your affliction, Jesus is not only with you — He has already walked through it for you.
Look, and live. Look, and rise.

Beloved, whatever fire you are walking through right now, know this: the Day of the Lord has come upon you — not to destroy, but to refine and resurrect. Look to the crucified and risen Jesus. The same God who healed Israel through the bronze serpent is healing you through the cross. Your suffering is not wasted. It is the very ground where resurrection power is breaking through.
